# Corporate Gig Strategy
**Breaking Into the High-Value Market**

---

## Overview

Corporate events are a goldmine — higher budgets, professional settings, and often repeat business. A single corporate gig ($1,500-3,000) = 3-4 restaurant gigs ($400-600 each).

**Goal:** Land 10+ corporate gigs by end of year 1. This gives you both revenue stability and prestige.

**Why Companies Hire Bands:**
- Team building/morale events
- Client entertainment
- Conference/trade show entertainment
- Holiday parties
- Corporate retreats
- Office celebrations

**How to Reach Them:**
- Search: "Event planner Sacramento", "Meeting planner Sacramento"

**How to Reach Them:**

### Channel 3: Venue Partnerships

**Smart Venues Often Refer Entertainment:**

Once you build relationships with restaurants/hotels, they often refer you to corporate event planners who use their venue.

**How to Activate This:**
- After each restaurant gig, ask manager: "Do you ever have corporate events or private functions?"
- Mention to restaurant managers: "If you ever have clients looking for entertainment, please keep us in mind"
- Create a one-page "Entertainment Flyer" for the venue to share with corporate clients

### Channel 4: GigSalad & Similar Platforms

**GigSalad, Thumbtack, WeddingWire** — online marketplaces where clients post gig requests.

**Pros:** Steady lead generation, large client base  
**Cons:** Commission 10-15%, lots of competition, need strong reviews

**Recommendation:** Join after you have 3-5 solid gigs (for testimonials).

### Channel 5: Networking & Referrals

**Most valuable source:** Word of mouth

**How to Build:**
- Ask every client: "Do you know anyone who might need us?"
- Offer referral bonus: "Bring us a referral that books → 10% off your next gig"
- Build genuine relationships with event professionals

## Corporate Positioning

Unlike restaurant gigs, corporate clients need to know you're **professional, reliable, and impressive**.

### Marketing Materials (Build These First)

1. **One-Page Corporate Entertainment Flyer**
   - Band photo
   - 2-3 sentence description
   - List of event types you serve
   - Sample setlist
   - Testimonials (once you have them)
   - Rates and contact info

2. **Video Demo Reel (30-60 seconds)**
   - Best clips from recent performances
   - Professional edit (not raw footage)
   - Showcases energy + sophistication
   - Upload to YouTube, embed in email

3. **Case Study / Testimonial Collection**
   - "Our corporate retreat was a huge hit thanks to Groove Therapy"
   - Include client name, event type, feedback
   - Build this over time as you book corporate gigs

4. **Website "Corporate Packages" Page**
   - List event types you serve
   - Pricing tiers (e.g., 2-hour set $1,500, 3-hour $2,000)
   - Customization options
   - Client testimonials

## Pricing Strategy for Corporate Gigs

**Typical Pricing:**

```
STANDARD RATES (3-piece band):

2-Hour Gig: $1,500
3-Hour Gig: $2,000
4-Hour Gig: $2,500
Full Day/Evening Event: $3,000-5,000

ADD-ONS:
- Cocktail Hour (1 hour): +$400
- MC/Hosting: +$200
- Special Requests (learn new song, etc.): +$300
- Travel (outside 10-mile radius): +$0.75/mile
- Setup/Breakdown (extra hour): +$300
```

**Why These Numbers?**
- Corporate budgets expect to pay $1,500+ minimum
- Underpricing makes you look unprofessional
- Higher price = perceived higher quality
- You can offer discounts for early booking or repeat business

**Negotiation:**
- Don't come down more than 10% max
- Offer value-adds instead (extra hour at same price, custom setlist, etc.)
- Stand firm on minimums ($1,500)

## Corporate Event Customization

Unlike a restaurant where you play your regular setlist, corporate clients want customized experiences.

### Questions to Ask Corporate Clients

1. **Event Type & Vibe**
   - "What's the tone of your event? Upscale/fun/formal/casual?"
   - "Will people be dancing or just listening/networking?"

2. **Audience Demographics**
   - "What's the age range of attendees?"
   - "Any specific genres they love?"

3. **Timing**
   - "When should we kick off? When should we wind down?"
   - "Any speeches or activities we need to work around?"

4. **Setlist**
   - "Any songs we MUST play?"
   - "Any songs to avoid?"
   - "Want recognizable standards, or more original material?"

5. **Technical**
   - "What sound system is available?"
   - "Is there a stage/dance floor?"
   - "Parking & load-in details?"

### Sample Corporate Setlist

For an upscale corporate event:
- Mix of classic jazz standards (familiar to older guests)
- Some contemporary smooth jazz (appealing to younger guests)
- Upbeat numbers (energy boosts)
- Slower pieces (for conversations/dining)
- Can include instrumental versions of popular songs (neutral, recognizable)

## Corporate Sales Process

### Week 1: Initial Contact
- Send introduction email with video & one-pager
- Wait 3-5 days, follow up if no response
- Be warm but professional

### Week 2: Phone/Video Call
- Discuss event details, client needs, setlist preferences
- Send proposal email with:
  - Event description (confirm what we discussed)
  - Rate & package details
  - Contract/agreement terms
  - Payment schedule (e.g., 50% deposit to book, 50% day of)

### Week 3-4: Close
- Follow up on proposal
- Answer any questions
- Get signed agreement + deposit
- Confirm logistics via email

### Post-Booking
- 60 days before: Send "pre-event planning" email confirming details
- 30 days before: Check in, finalize setlist
- 14 days before: Confirm final headcount & logistics
- Day before: Confirm arrival time, parking, contact info
- Day of: Arrive early, set up, deliver great performance
- Day after: Thank you email with photos, request testimonial

## Building Long-Term Corporate Relationships

### The Residency Model

Instead of one-off gigs, propose recurring entertainment:

"We'd love to be your band for [Company]'s quarterly team events. Let's set up a residency agreement for 2-3 events per year."

**Benefits:**
- Predictable revenue
- Easier logistics (you know the venue, clients, expectations)
- Relationship deepens
- Can negotiate better rates for commitment

**Example Residency:**
- 3 events per year (holiday party, summer retreat, client appreciation)
- $2,000 per event ($6,000/year commitment)
- Company gets 10% discount if they book all 3 at once

### Referral Incentive

Once you deliver a great corporate gig, ask for referrals:

"We loved playing for your team. If you know other companies that might want entertainment, we'd love to chat with them — and we'd be happy to give you a discount on your next event as a thank you for the referral."

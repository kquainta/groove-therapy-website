# Venue Booking Strategy
**Building a 2 Gigs/Month Pipeline**

---

## Overview

The foundation of Groove Therapy's business is live performances. This strategy focuses on identifying and booking venues in the Greater Sacramento area with the goal of **2-3 gigs per month** with opportunities for premium travel gigs.

**Goal:** 8-10 gigs in year 1, 24+ in year 2.

---

## Target Venue Types

### Primary Targets (Higher Priority)

#### 1. **Restaurants & Lounges**
- Wine bars, upscale casual dining
- Happy hour entertainment (5-9pm)
- Friday/Saturday dinner sets

**Why:** Regular bookings, built-in audience, good pay ($300-600/night)

**Examples in Sacramento:**
- Mulvaney's B&L, Ella Dining Room & Bar, Manifest Wine Bar

#### 2. **Listening Rooms & Jazz Clubs**
- Dedicated live music venues
- Jazz-focused events
- Community atmosphere

**Why:** Perfect audience, prestige, can build residency deals

**Examples:**
- The Pocket, Harlow's, Old Ironsides

#### 3. **Hotels & Resorts**
- Lobby bars, restaurant venues
- Weekend brunch performances
- Conferences/events

**Why:** Corporate backing, steady work, good rates

**Examples:**
- Sheraton, InterContinental, local boutique hotels

### Secondary Targets (Occasional/Strategic)

#### 4. **Wedding Venues & Event Spaces**
- Private events, rehearsal dinners
- Higher pay ($500-1500/night)
- Requires custom setlists

#### 5. **Coffee Shops & Cafes**
- Daytime/early evening (10am-2pm)
- Lower pay but brand building
- Good for community relationships

#### 6. **Charity & Community Events**
- Festivals, fundraisers, community celebrations
- Lower pay but high visibility
- Can be lucrative with sponsored events

## Venue Research & Outreach

### Step 1: Build Your Venue List

Create a spreadsheet with:
- Venue name
- Address & phone
- Website & social media
- Decision maker contact (manager, entertainment director, owner)
- Music genre they usually book
- Past success (if any)
- Last contact date
- Status (interested, rejected, pending response, booked)

[Template: VENUE_TRACKER.csv]

**How to Find Venues:**
- Google: "Jazz venues Sacramento", "restaurants live music Sacramento"
- Yelp: Filter by "live music" + location
- Facebook: Search local event groups, venue pages
- Local music FB groups: Ask musicians where they play
- Tourism board: Sacramento CVB has event venue lists

**Target:** Build a list of 50+ venues by Month 2.

### Step 2: Craft Your Outreach

**Cold Email/Call Template:**

```
Subject: Live Smooth Jazz Performance for [Venue]

Hi [Manager Name],

I'm reaching out because [Venue] seems like the perfect place for Groove Therapy, Sacramento's smooth jazz band. We specialize in upscale, audience-friendly performances that enhance the dining/bar experience without overwhelming conversation.

We're available for:
- Friday/Saturday dinner sets (6-10pm)
- Happy hour entertainment (5-8pm)
- Weekend brunch (10am-1pm)
- Private events & celebrations

You can hear us here: [Website/YouTube link]

Would you be open to a short phone conversation to discuss possibilities? No pressure — we're always looking to find the right fit.

Best,
Kevin Quaintance
Groove Therapy
q@groovetherapy.live
[Phone]
```

**Key Points:**
- Personalize with venue name & decision maker name (research them first)
- Explain why YOU'RE a good fit (not why THEY should help you)
- Offer multiple time slots (flexibility = bookability)
- Include proof of quality (videos, links)
- Make it easy to say yes
- Follow up after 1 week if no response

### Step 3: The Phone Call

When they call back (or you reach them):

**Goal:** 15-minute conversation that results in either:
a) A gig booked
b) Agreement to trial performance (free or reduced rate)
c) Contact info + future consideration

**Talking Points:**
- "What kind of music does your venue typically feature?"
- "What time slots are you looking to fill?"
- "What's your typical audience size and vibe?"
- "What's your budget for entertainment?"
- "Can we do a trial performance or happy hour slot to show you what we're about?"

**Close:**
- "I'd love to set up a time that works for you. How about [specific Friday evening]?"
- "Let's start with a happy hour set and see how your guests respond"
- "When's a good time for you to grab a quick call this week?"

## Booking Strategy by Stage

### Stage 1: Market Penetration (Months 1-3)

**Goal:** Get 4-6 gigs on the calendar to build momentum.

**Tactics:**
- Cold email 20+ venues (10 restaurants, 5 lounges, 5 hotels)
- Offer "trial" or reduced rate for first booking ($200-300 vs standard $500)
- Focus on venues that explicitly book live music
- Emphasize flexibility and professionalism

**Success Metric:** 1-2 gigs per month

### Stage 2: Relationship Building (Months 4-6)

**Goal:** Convert trial bookings into repeat bookings. Build residencies.

**Tactics:**
- Follow up after every gig with manager (thank you + photo)
- Offer residency deals: "Play here every other Friday for 6 months"
- Ask venues for referrals to other venues
- Track performance metrics (crowd size, drink sales impact, feedback)

**Residency Example:**
- Every other Friday, 7-10pm @ [Venue]
- Rate: $400/night
- 6-month commitment (12 gigs)
- Guaranteed revenue: $4,800

**Success Metric:** 2+ gigs per month, 1-2 residencies forming

### Stage 3: Premium Opportunities (Months 7-12)

**Goal:** Higher-paying gigs, wedding/corporate events, occasional travel.

**Tactics:**
- Pursue wedding venue partnerships
- Contact corporate event planners
- Target mid-tier hotels for conference entertainment
- Negotiate higher rates ($600-1000+) with established venues
- Accept 1-2 travel gigs if pay is $1,500+

**Success Metric:** 2+ gigs/month, average rate $500+, 1-2 premium gigs

## Rate Setting

**Typical Rates:**

| Venue Type | Rate | Notes |
|------------|------|-------|
| **Restaurant/Lounge** | $300-600 | Depends on venue size, audience |
| **Hotel Bar/Brunch** | $400-700 | Corporate backing, larger audience |
| **Jazz Club** | $400-800 | Music-focused, potentially split tips |
| **Wedding/Private Event** | $800-1500+ | Premium, custom setlist |
| **Coffee Shop/Community** | $100-300 | Brand building, low pressure |
| **Festival/Charity** | $200-500 | Depends on sponsorship |

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Don't undersell (builds expectation you're cheap)
- Offer discounts for recurring/long-term deals only
- Include what's provided: How many band members? Equipment? Amplification?
- Get it in writing (email confirmation minimum)

## Contract Essentials

Every booking should have a written agreement (doesn't need to be fancy):

**Email Confirmation Should Include:**
```
BOOKING CONFIRMATION — Groove Therapy

Venue: [Name]
Date: [Date]
Time: [Start-End Time]
Load-in: [Time]
Equipment Provided: [Venue sound system, stage, chairs? Or does band provide?]
Rate: $[amount]
Payment Terms: [Cash at end of gig / Invoice with 30-day terms]
Setlist: [Requests or open to your standards?]
Cancellation Policy: [72-hour cancellation = full refund, <72h = 50%]

Contact: [Manager name & phone]

Confirmed by: [Manager signature/email approval]
```

**Key Protections:**
- Written confirmation (email counts)
- Clear payment amount and timing
- Cancellation policy (protects you both)
- Equipment expectations (so no surprises on gig day)

## Performance Maximization

Once you're booked, deliver excellence:

### Pre-Gig
- Confirm details 48-72 hours before (time, address, contact person)
- Prepare setlist based on venue vibe
- Check equipment needs with venue
- Arrive 30 minutes early for sound check

### During Gig
- Professional appearance (matching or coordinated outfit)
- Engage with audience (greetings, thank yous, warm energy)
- Respect the room (not too loud, read the crowd)
- Monitor for technical issues early

### Post-Gig
- Thank the manager/venue during gig
- Follow up with email within 48 hours:
  - "Loved playing for you and your guests"
  - Attach a photo or video clip
  - "Would love to do this again — open to recurring bookings"
  - Link to mailing list (ask audience to sign up)

## Seasonal Strategy

**Spring (March-May):** Wedding season starts, conferences, outdoor events  
**Summer (June-Aug):** Festivals, outdoor venues, vacation season (fewer bookings, target tourists)  
**Fall (Sept-Nov):** Strong season, corporate events pick up, festival season  
**Winter (Dec-Jan):** Holiday parties (high pay), then slow in Jan  

**Adjust outreach intensity seasonally:**
- Ramp up corporate outreach in Aug (for Q4 holiday parties)
- Focus on wedding venues in Jan-Feb (spring weddings)
- Target festivals in June-July

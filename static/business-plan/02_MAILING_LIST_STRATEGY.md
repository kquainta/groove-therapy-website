# Mailing List Growth Strategy
**Building & Maintaining Your Direct Fan Channel**

---

## Overview

Your mailing list is the most valuable asset you can build. Unlike social media (which algorithms control), your email list is **yours**. People on your list have explicitly opted in to hear from you.

**Goal:** Grow from 0 → 500+ engaged subscribers in 12 months.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

### 1.1 Choose Your Platform

**Recommended:** Mailchimp (freemium, up to 500 contacts) or ConvertKit (more flexible, better for creators)

| Platform | Cost | Best For | Notes |
|----------|------|----------|-------|
| **Mailchimp** | Free up to 500 | Simple automation, growing list | Easy to start, limited design |
| **ConvertKit** | $25-80/mo | Creator/artist focus | Better segmentation, landing pages |
| **Custom** | Varies | Full control | Requires dev work (Lenny can help) |

**Decision:** Start with **Mailchimp free tier** to validate interest, migrate to ConvertKit if you hit 500+ subscribers.

### 1.2 Build Website Signup Form

The #1 way people join your list is through your website.

**Specs:**
- Simple landing page with signup form
- Clear value prop: "Get tour dates, behind-the-scenes content, and monthly jazz tips"
- Email validation (prevent spam)
- Double opt-in (send confirmation email)
- Mobile-friendly
- Privacy: Link to privacy policy

**Form Fields:**
- Email (required)
- First Name (optional but encouraged)
- Location/Zip Code (optional - helps target local gigs)

**Form Integration:**
- Mailchimp API or form embed
- Auto-subscribe to welcome sequence

[See: TOOL_MAILING_LIST_SPEC.md for technical setup]

### 1.3 Create Welcome Sequence (Automated)

When someone signs up, they should immediately feel like they made the right choice.

**Welcome Email 1 (immediate):**
- Thank you message
- What to expect (monthly newsletter, gig announcements)
- Direct link to band's social media

**Welcome Email 2 (day 3):**
- Band's story (who you are, why you play jazz)
- Link to best performance video or clip
- Ask them to follow social media

**Welcome Email 3 (day 7):**
- Exclusive content: Behind-the-scenes band photo or audio snippet
- Next upcoming gig (if available)

## Phase 2: Growth Channels (Ongoing)

### 2.1 Social Media CTAs

Every Instagram/Facebook post should have a call-to-action to join the mailing list.

**Sample CTAs:**
- "Want gig announcements first? Join our mailing list: [link]"
- "Follow us here + sign up for monthly jazz insights: [link]"
- "Subscribe for exclusive behind-the-scenes content: [link]"

**Cadence:** Every 3-4 posts, include a CTA (not every post — that's annoying)

### 2.2 At-Venue Sign-Up Strategy

Set up a physical sign-up sheet at every gig.

**What You Need:**
- Printed signup form (simple: Email + Name + City)
- iPad/tablet with QR code linking to online form (better UX)
- Pen + clipboard

**Process:**
- Place near venue entrance or at band's merch table
- Mention during performance: "Sign up for tour dates and exclusive content"
- Enter paper signups into Mailchimp weekly

**QR Code:** Create a custom QR code pointing to your mailing list landing page. Print on flyers, posters, band materials.

### 2.3 Organic Growth

- Ask fans to refer friends (monthly raffle: enter 1 raffle per friend referred)
- Include signup link in email signature (band email)
- Add link to band bio/website

### 2.4 Paid Growth (Optional, Month 4+)

Once you have a solid welcome sequence, consider Facebook ads targeting Sacramento area.

**Budget:** $50-100/month
**Goal:** Cost per subscriber < $1.00
**Target:** People interested in jazz, live music, Sacramento events

## Phase 3: Engagement & Retention (Monthly)

### 3.1 Monthly Newsletter

**Cadence:** 1st of every month (consistent, predictable)

**Content Mix** (for a 3-4 minute read):
1. **Upcoming Gigs** (most important) — dates, venues, ticket links
2. **Behind-the-Scenes** (1-2 sentences + photo) — band story, practice session, new songs
3. **Jazz Tip or Story** (educational) — interesting fact about jazz history, a musician spotlight, or band member Q&A
4. **Call-to-Action** — follow social media, bring a friend to next gig, reply with feedback

**Template:**
```
Subject: [Band Name] News: March Gigs + Behind-the-Scenes

Hi [FirstName],

🎵 **This Month's Performances**
- March 15 @ [Venue]: Doors at 7pm, Music at 8pm [Get Tickets]
- March 28 @ [Venue]: Happy Hour Special, 5-9pm [Learn More]

🎬 **Behind the Scenes**
[Photo] We spent last week writing two new originals! Check out our Instagram for a snippet.

📚 **Jazz Spotlight: The Birth of Smooth Jazz**
Did you know smooth jazz grew out of fusion in the 1970s? Artists like Grover Washington Jr. pioneered...
[Read more on our blog]

🎸 **Bring a Friend!**
Know someone who loves live music? Bring them to our next show — we'd love to meet them.

See you soon,
Groove Therapy
```

### 3.2 Engagement Metrics

Track in Mailchimp:
- **Open Rate** (goal: >30%)
- **Click-Through Rate** (goal: >5%)
- **Unsubscribe Rate** (goal: <0.5%)
- **Bounce Rate** (keep <2%)

**Monthly Review:** Look at what content performed best. More video links? More gig info? More stories? Adjust next month.

### 3.3 Segmentation (Future)

Once you hit 200+ subscribers, segment your list:
- **Local** (Sacramento area) — emphasize local gigs
- **Remote** — emphasize recordings, virtual performances
- **Highly Engaged** — send bonus content

## Phase 4: Monetization (Month 6+)

As your list grows, consider:
- **Exclusive Content** for subscribers (acoustic recordings, live session videos)
- **Early Ticket Sales** to mailing list (48h before public)
- **Merch Links** (t-shirts, CDs, vinyl)
- **Fan Supporter Tier** (Patreon-style: $3-5/mo for bonus content)

## Troubleshooting

**Q: No one's signing up.**
- A: Your value prop might be unclear. Test different copy on your signup form. Make sure CTAs are visible on social media.

**Q: High unsubscribe rate (>2%).**
- A: Your emails might be too frequent or irrelevant. Stick to 1x/month. Focus on gig announcements.

**Q: Low open rates (<20%).**
- A: Your subject lines might be weak. Test different angles: "Groove Therapy Tour Dates Inside" vs "Your March Jazz Lineup".

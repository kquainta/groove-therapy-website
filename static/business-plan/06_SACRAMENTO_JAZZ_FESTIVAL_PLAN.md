# Sacramento Jazz Festival Plan
**Building a Flagship Event for Groove Therapy**

---

## Vision

Create a **one-day Sacramento Jazz Festival** featuring local and regional jazz artists, food vendors, and a fun, accessible atmosphere. This builds Groove Therapy's brand, creates revenue, and becomes a signature annual event.

**Target Timeline:** Fall 2026 OR Spring 2027

---

## Festival Concept

### Event Details

**Format:** Outdoor one-day music festival

**When:** Saturday afternoon/evening, 2pm-9pm

**Where:** Sacramento riverfront, Discovery Park, or similar civic space

**Capacity:** 500-1,500 attendees (depending on venue)

**Admission:** $15-25 per person (free kids under 12)

**Acts:** 6-8 jazz acts (mix of headliners and local bands, including Groove Therapy)

**Vendors:** Food trucks, beverages, craft vendors

## Venue Selection

### Must-Have Features

- **Parking:** 200+ spaces or nearby public parking
- **Accessibility:** ADA compliant, easy entry/exit
- **Weather:** Covered or tent-able area for backup
- **Power:** Adequate electrical for sound system
- **Restrooms:** Public facilities on-site
- **Space:** Room for 6-8 performance/vendor areas
- **Public Appeal:** Central location, easy to find

### Top Sacramento Venues to Consider

1. **Discovery Park** (American River Parkway)
   - Large, scenic, established venue for events
   - City permits required but manageable
   - Great capacity (can handle 1,000+)

2. **Old Sacramento Waterfront**
   - Historic, charming, high foot traffic
   - May have usage restrictions
   - Premium location = higher rental

3. **Southside Park**
   - Green space, good infrastructure
   - Community-friendly location
   - Moderate cost

4. **Raley Field/Sacramento Convention Center Area**
   - Downtown proximity
   - Existing event infrastructure
   - Indoor fallback option

**Recommendation:** Start with Discovery Park (free or low-cost for non-profits, if Groove Therapy incorporates)

## Artist Recruitment

### Types of Acts to Approach

**Headliners (1-2 acts):**
- Established regional smooth jazz artists
- Names that draw crowds (35+ year-olds primarily)
- Pay: $1,000-2,000 each
- Book 3-4 months ahead

**Local/Mid-Tier Bands (3-4 acts):**
- Sacramento-based jazz musicians
- Up-and-coming groups
- Pay: $300-800 each
- Book 2-3 months ahead

**Rising Acts (1-2 acts):**
- Newer local talent (lower pay, high enthusiasm)
- Pay: $100-300 + exposure
- Recruitment: Easy if enthusiastic

## Festival Day Logistics

### Pre-Event Setup (8am-1pm)

**Crew Responsibilities:**
- Sound/stage setup (AV team)
- Vendor setup (vendor coordinator)
- Registration/ticket area (2 people)
- Signage and wayfinding
- Parking assistance

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Sound system tested
- [ ] Microphones + monitors working
- [ ] Artists briefed on schedule/sound check
- [ ] Vendors ready
- [ ] Staff/volunteer briefed on roles
- [ ] Weather backup plan confirmed

### Event Day (2pm-9pm)

**Rough Schedule:**
- 2:00pm — Festival opens, first act takes stage
- 2:30-3:15pm — Act 1
- 3:15-3:45pm — Break, food/vendor time
- 3:45-4:30pm — Act 2
- 4:30-5:30pm — Food/social time
- 5:30-6:15pm — Act 3
- 6:15-7:00pm — Act 4
- 7:00-7:15pm — Break
- 7:15-8:00pm — Act 5 (Headliner)
- 8:00-8:30pm — Groove Therapy (as headliners or closing act)
- 8:30-9:00pm — Final act / Goodbye

**Staffing:**
- MC/Host (Kevin or volunteer)
- Sound tech
- Stage manager
- Registration/ticket area (2 people)
- Vendor coordinator
- Parking/traffic
- Roaming staff (answer questions, manage issues)

### Post-Event (9pm+)

- Thank all performers
- Take down setup
- Collect feedback from attendees
- Thank sponsors/vendors
- Document with photos/video
- Calculate final numbers
